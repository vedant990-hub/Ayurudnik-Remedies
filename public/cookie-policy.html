What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that your browser stores on your device when you visit a website. They're a standard part of how the internet works and help websites remember information about you.

When you visit our website again, these cookies are sent back to us. This helps us understand your browsing patterns and improve your experience.

How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for a straightforward purpose: to understand how people use our website so we can make it better.

Specifically, we track:

  • Which pages you visit
  • How long you spend on each page
  • Where visitors come from (search engines, direct links, etc.)
  • General location (country/region level, not street address)
  • Type of device you're using

This information helps us see what's working and what could be improved, so we can give you a better website experience.

Analytics

We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors interact with our website. Google Analytics collects information such as pages visited, time spent on the site, and browser/device information. This data is collected in an aggregated and anonymized manner and is used solely to improve our website and services.

We do not use Google Analytics to collect personally identifiable information.

Analytics with Google

We use Google Analytics to understand website traffic and user behavior. This is our only external tracking tool.

What Google Analytics Does

  • Counts how many people visit our website
  • Shows us which pages are most popular
  • Tells us how visitors found our site
  • Tracks basic device and location information
Important: Google Analytics does not track your personal identity. We don't use it to identify who you are, and we don't combine it with personal data from forms or accounts.

Google's Privacy

Google has its own privacy policies that govern how it processes analytics data. You can learn more at Google's Privacy Policy.

What We Don't Track

To be clear, here's what we do not use cookies for:

  • ❌ Advertising or retargeting (showing you ads on other websites)
  • ❌ Profiling or creating detailed user profiles
  • ❌ Third-party tracking beyond Google Analytics
  • ❌ Collecting sensitive personal information
  • ❌ Marketing to you based on your browsing behavior
Summary: We only use Google Analytics to understand general website traffic patterns. We don't sell your data, create profiles about you, or use cookies for advertising purposes.
